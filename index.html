About Dan

I’m Daniel, born and raised in Scotland, and Edinburgh is the place I call home. I like showing people the city in a way that feels relaxed, funny, and memorable—more like wandering with a friend than following a script.

Expect some sarcasm, plenty of laughs, and a few wee facts you’ll actually remember.

The essentials

  • • Max 4 people (families of 5 on request)
  • • Language: English
  • • Pace: relaxed; hills and cobbles included
  • • Meeting point: “Centre of Edinburgh” bollard (Waterloo Place)

The Tour

We start at the (underwhelming) “centre of Edinburgh” bollard, head up Calton Hill for the views, then wander the Old Town and Grassmarket with a pint stop along the way. Short stories, daft details, and time for photos.

  • • Calton Hill (Nelson Monument, National Monument, Playfair)
  • • Old Town closes & viewpoints (towards Leith)
  • • Grassmarket & Royal Mile
  • • Portuguese cannon cameo

Good to know

  • • Toilets and pub stop built into the route.
  • • Not heavy on dates; light, human stories instead.
  • • Wet-weather adjustments if needed.

Why Join

  • • Tiny groups—feels personal, not crowded.
  • • Stories you’ll remember (and repeat in the pub later).
  • • A guide who sounds like a person, not a brochure.

A wee note

Disclaimer: If you’re after exact dates, long timelines, and flawless historical accuracy, I’m not the Dan for you. There are tours that’ll give you that. This one’s for a laugh, a wander, a pint, and just enough history to tick the tourism box.

FAQ

Is this a history-heavy tour?
No. You’ll get the gist and the good stories—short and memorable—without a lecture.
Do I have to drink?
No. We stop at a pub for a sit-down; you can grab a soft drink, tea, or nothing at all.
Is it suitable for kids?
Aye. Content and language adjusted to the group. Families of five can enquire by email.
Where do we meet?
At the “Centre of Edinburgh” bollard on Waterloo Place. It’s a literal bollard—hard to miss.
